# If I Started on LinkedIn from Zero - Full Strategy

If I started on LinkedIn from zero, here's what I'd do. Most LinkedIn advice comes from people who built their audience in 2022 when the platform was a complete ghost town. They'll tell you to just post consistently and be authentic. Use storytelling. Here's the problem. I run a content agency. We've built LinkedIn presences for founders and executives with massive logos behind their name and founders with logos that aren't that recognized across different industries, different ICPs. And I can tell you with certainty what works for one would completely fail for the other.

So if I had to start from zero today in 2026, knowing everything I know from actually doing this work every single day, here's exactly what I'd do.

## Mindset Shift: LinkedIn as a Distribution Channel

Stop thinking about LinkedIn like social media. For B2B companies, LinkedIn is a distribution channel. It's where your buyers are already spending time making decisions and vetting vendors before they ever hit your website. The question isn't, should we be on LinkedIn? The question is, are we going to own that channel or let our competitors own it?

Your prospects are scrolling LinkedIn. They're searching for solutions. They're reading content from people in your space. They're forming opinions about who knows what they are talking about and who doesn't. If you are not in that conversation, someone else is.

## Step 1: 72 Hours of Strategic Research (Before Posting Anything)

Before I post a single thing, I'm spending 72 hours doing something most people skip entirely. I'm going to consume - not mindlessly scroll. I mean strategic, intentional research.

Find 20 to 30 people in my space who are doing well. Not the mega influencers with 500,000 followers. I'm looking for people with 5K to 30K followers who are clearly getting engagement from the right people. Study them like a case study:
- What topics are they hitting over and over?
- What hooks are they using?
- What formats - text posts, carousels, video?
- How are they ending their posts?

LinkedIn has niches just like YouTube. There are unwritten rules about what works in B2B SaaS versus what works in executive coaching versus what works in marketing. Spend 72 hours learning the game before you play it.

## Step 2: Profile as a Landing Page

Your headline should not just be your job title. Every word is intentional. Example: "Building content machines | Founder at distinctiva.io, content production house | Content strategist | Speaker"

- **Banner and photo:** Clear, professional, approachable. Color consistency builds brand recognition.
- **About section formula:** Problem, solution, proof, CTA. Not your life story.
- **Featured section:** Link to places where people can convert - website, newsletter. Don't pin random posts.
- **Custom CTA button:** Link to your website, newsletter, whatever your next step is.

Test: If a stranger lands on your profile and can't explain what you do within 10 seconds, fix it before you post anything.

## LinkedIn SEO Tips

LinkedIn is the number two most cited source for AI. SEO your profile:
- LinkedIn newsletters rank on Google - treat them like blog posts
- Change your image alt text to keyword-friendly descriptions
- Name your carousel files with SEO-friendly titles before uploading
- Add keywords to your headline, about section, and services section
- Use keywords naturally in posts - write for humans, be searchable-friendly

## Content Funnel: Four Buckets

1. **Growth content** - Broad insights that expand reach. Industry observations, newsjacking, trendjacking, pattern recognition, hot takes.
2. **Authority content** - Frameworks, case studies, client results, deep expertise. Makes people think "these people get it."
3. **Conversion content** - Lead magnets, direct offers, newsletter signups, CTAs that move people from engaged followers to qualified conversations.
4. **Personal content** - If you've built a community, people want to know what's going on.

Your point of view should be woven through all of them. Growth brings in people. Authority builds trust. Conversion creates pipeline. Point of view makes you memorable. That's the flywheel.

## Content Extraction System

Executives say "I don't have time to create content." You don't need to create content - you need to extract it.

- Sales calls - every objection you handle, every question you answer, that's content
- Strategy docs - frameworks built internally, processes that actually work
- Client conversations - patterns you're seeing across accounts

Record calls with Fathom, connect to automation tools. Every sales conversation becomes raw material for LinkedIn content in your own voice.

## Thought Leadership vs. Content Creation

There's a real difference between:
- A **content creator** - knows how to work the algorithm, understands hooks and formats
- A **thought leader** - has a genuine point of view shaped by experience, has done the work
- A **subject matter expert** - holds the real insight, been in the trenches, knows what actually works

Most B2B teams don't have a thought leadership gap. They have a courage gap. Thought leadership doesn't come from publishing more. It comes from sitting in discomfort long enough to extract a defensible point of view from someone who would rather be left alone. Most teams skip that part - too awkward, too slow, too risky. So they ship vibes instead of substance.

## Comments: The Highest Leverage Activity in Your First 90 Days

When you post to zero followers, nobody sees it. But when you comment on a post with 50,000 impressions, you're borrowing that creator's audience.

A good comment does one of three things:
1. Adds value - share an experience, resource, a different angle
2. Challenges respectfully - disagree with nuance and invite discussion
3. Tells a quick story that relates to the topic

Spend 30 minutes every single day commenting before thinking about your own posts. Comment on posts from people whose audiences match your ICP. This is how you get discovered, not burnt out.

## Your Actual Point of View

The one thing to obsess over more than anything else: finding your actual point of view. Not what sounds smart, not what's working for other people - your actual genuine perspective on your space.

- What do you believe that goes against what most people say?
- Where do you think the industry is wrong?
- What have you learned from experience that contradicts popular advice?

This is your moat. This is what makes people follow you instead of the hundred other people posting about the same topics.

## Consistency and Timing

Five posts per week (Mon-Fri). If just starting, three times minimum. Pick a cadence you can sustain for 6 months. Lock it into the calendar.

Best timing: 7 to 9am Eastern, Tuesday through Thursday for B2B. Sundays also perform well - that's when decision makers check LinkedIn before their calendars fill up.

## What NOT to Do

- Don't join pods
- Don't post and ghost - engage with every comment, especially in the first hour
- Don't wait until you're ready - your first 50 posts will be mediocre and that's fine
- The people who win are the ones who post through the awkward and cringe phase
