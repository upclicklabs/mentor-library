# Neil Patel: How AI Agents Will Reshape Commerce (The Third Internet Shift)

In the next 12 months, millions of people will stop searching for products and start letting AI agents buy for them. The companies they choose will see a massive surge in revenue. The companies they skip will never even know they were in the running. This is the biggest shift in how customers find businesses since the invention of Google and almost no one is talking about it.

I'm Neil Patel. I'm the co-founder of NP Digital. We're the performance marketing agency of the year by Ad Age and in this video, I'm going to show you how AI agents actually shop. How they scan, compare, and decide who gets the sale. Then, I'll walk through exactly what to change on your website so these agents choose you every time.

## The Three Massive Shifts

There have been exactly two massive shifts in how people find businesses online.

**The first was the desktop internet.** Google launched and suddenly, if your business showed up on page one, you won. The companies that learned SEO early dominated for a decade before their competitors even understood what happened.

**The second was mobile.** Smartphones changed everything. The businesses that went mobile first - responsive sites, fast load times, apps - captured market share they still hold today. The ones that waited, a lot of them never recovered.

**Right now, we're standing at the beginning of the third shift.** This one's different. Because the first two shifts changed how people search. This shift changes who is doing the search.

It's not your customer browsing your website anymore, it's their AI agent scanning your site, pulling your data, comparing you against 30 competitors in a second, and making a recommendation before a human ever sees your page.

This isn't coming in 5 years. ChatGPT already has shopping features. Perplexity is recommending products. Google is building AI agents directly into Chrome. It's happening now.

## The New Customer Journey

This shift doesn't change how people find you, it rewrites the entire customer journey from scratch.

For 25 years, the customer journey looked the same. Someone has a problem, they search Google, they click a few links, they browse some websites, they compare options, they make a decision. Humans were doing the work at every single stage. That's ending.

Here's what's happening instead. Someone tells your AI agent, "Find me the best marketing agency for my e-commerce brand under 5,000 a month." The agent goes out, visits dozens of websites, pulls pricing, services, reviews, case studies, cross references that against brand mentions and third-party data, comes back with three recommendations. The human picks one. Done. They never visited a single site.

The entire funnel - awareness, consideration, decision - got compressed into one AI conversation. The website didn't need to convince a human, it needed to convince an agent. And that agent doesn't care about your beautiful design, doesn't care about your brand colors, or your clever headline. It cares about one thing: can I extract clear, structured information from this site that helps my human make a decision? If yes, you make the short list. If no, you don't exist.

## What AI Agents Are Looking For

AI agents don't read your website the way a person does. A person scrolls, scans, gets a feel for your brand. An agent scans your code looking for specific signals.

**1. Structured data, schema markup, and JSON-LD.** This is the language AI agents speak. It tells them exactly what your page is about, what you sell, what it costs, and who it's for. Without it, you're invisible.

**2. Content clarity.** Not clever marketing copy - clear, specific, machine-readable information. What do you offer? How does it work? What's the pricing? If the answers are buried in five paragraphs of brand storytelling, the agent skips you.

The same things that make your site accessible to people with disabilities - ARIA tags, clean HTML structure, clear labels - those are now the exact same things that make your site visible to AI agents. Accessibility for humans became visibility for AI.

**3. API compatibility.** Agents aren't just reading text, they're looking for connections they can plug into. If an AI can check your inventory, pull pricing, or book an appointment through a clean API, you win. If it has to guess by scraping your homepage, you lose. Google just launched something called the Universal Commerce Protocol that lets an agent handle everything from discovery to checkout. The sites that plug into this kind of thing first get the sales.

**4. Your reputation across the web.** Agents don't just read your website, they cross reference everything. Brand mentions, reviews, sentiment, citations, and other content. If no one else on the internet is talking about you, the agent doesn't trust you.

**5. Freshness.** When was this information last updated? AI agents heavily favor current data. If your pages look stale, you look unreliable.

Most of these signals are the same things that helped you rank in traditional SEO. The difference is, for AI agents, these aren't nice-to-haves, they're requirements. Miss one and you're out.

## The Five Changes to Make Your Website AI Agent Ready

**Step one: Add schema markup to every important page.** Product schema, service schema, FAQ schema, review schema. This is the single highest impact thing you can do. It takes an afternoon and it makes every AI agent on the internet able to understand exactly what you offer. Think of it like putting a name tag at a networking event. Except the event has a thousand people and everyone without a name tag is invisible.

**Step two: Rewrite your key pages for clarity, not cleverness.** Every page should answer four questions in plain language: What do you sell? Who's it for? What does it cost? How does it work? Headers, short paragraphs, tables where it makes sense. You're writing for a very smart, very literal reader with zero patience.

**Step three: Open your site to agent interactions.** If you sell products, make sure your inventory and pricing are accessible through clean data feeds or APIs. If you sell services, make sure your scheduling and availability is easy for an agent to query. The businesses that let AI agents plug in directly - check stock, compare specs, and even complete a purchase - those are the ones that get recommended first.

**Step four: Build your brand signal everywhere.** Get mentioned in industry publications. Earn reviews on relevant platforms. Create content other sites want to reference. AI agents trust you more when the rest of the internet vouches for you.

**Step five: Keep everything current.** Add last updated dates to your pages. Refresh your core content quarterly. Update your reviews and case studies. Stale content is a trust killer for agents.

**Step six (the long-term advantage): Create content that teaches AI who you are and what category you own.** Don't just say, "We're a marketing agency." Say, "We help SaaS companies scale from 1 million to 10 million through performance marketing." Create comparison pages. Build category-specific guides. Publish listicles that position you as authority in your niche, if you really are the authority in your niche. Don't lie. The specificity trains the AI. The more specific and consistent you are, the more AI agents associate your brand with that category. And once you own that association, that's when the real compounding starts.

## The Window Is Smaller Than Ever

Every major internet shift has had a window - a period where the early movers locked in advantages that lasted years. The businesses that figured out SEO in 2003, some still rank on page one. The brands that went mobile first in 2010, they captured market share they hold today.

The generative web has a window too, and it's smaller than any window that came before it. AI adoption is moving faster than anything we've seen. Gartner is predicting that by 2028, a massive percentage of both B2B and B2C transactions will involve AI agents in the decision. What took mobile 5 years to reach mainstream adoption, AI agents will do it in 12 to 18 months.

That means the early mover window isn't 3 to 5 years. It's measured in months.

Once AI agents learn which brands to recommend in a category, those recommendations compound. More recommendations lead to more traffic. More traffic leads to more data. More data leads to more authority. More authority leads to more recommendations. It's a flywheel, and the businesses that get on it first spin faster every single day. The businesses that wait are fighting an uphill battle against competitors who are already the default recommendation.

That's the real opportunity. Not AI content tools. Not ChatGPT tricks. The real play is making sure that when AI agents start doing the buying - and they already are - they're buying from you.

Most of your competitors won't watch this video. Most won't even take action even if they do. That's your advantage. Use it.
