# The Boomerang Method — Getting a Consistent 5% Reply Rate with Cold Email

If you're running an automated cold email campaign, you've probably noticed this one core problem: you get a decent amount of replies, but hardly any of those replies lead to actual booked calls and closed deals. In this video, I'll be breaking down the exact strategy I'm using inside my own cold email campaigns using a brand new angle that's getting me a consistent 5% reply rate, so you can just copy what I'm doing in your own business.

What I call the boomerang method has been absolutely crushing it for us in many different ways. I'm going to break this down in three sections, pretending I'm an ads agency that does paid ads for people — I can serve anyone anywhere, set up their whole campaigns, run ads, help them get more clients.

## Section 1: The List — Relevance Over Volume

What I used to do when I first started using cold email and trying to scale: get a list of 100,000 people that could potentially need help and blast them with the same message. The problem: there's no relevance.

**The shift:** Rather than building a super large list, build a list where you can be super relevant. Instead of pulling the biggest list of 10,000-20,000 people, be hyper niche.

### Example: Targeting Dentists

Using Instantly Super Search:
- Job titles: CEO, Owner, Founder, Co-Founder
- Industry keywords: dentist, dentistry, dental
- Result: 33,000 matching contacts

Then enrich the profiles:
- Click "find emails" → fully enriched profile
- Contact info, company description, company headcount, LinkedIn profile
- Work email verified through strong verification process
- Confidence that the email will actually land in their inbox

## Section 2: The Copy — The Three Ideas Strategy

When building lists, build them with the intention to write copy that's actually going to be relevant for who you're reaching out to.

### The Two Most Important Things in Copy

1. **Relevance** — the person receiving the email thinks "wow, this was written specifically for me"
2. **Value** — people don't know you, don't trust you. The best way to build trust is through adding value that's actually relevant.

### The Three Ideas Strategy

Provide your ideal customer with three ideas on how they can get to the outcome. If you're a website agency, provide three ideas on how they can optimize their website to increase conversion.

#### Using AI to Generate Personalized Ideas at Scale

In Instantly, add a column → Use AI:

**Example prompt for dental ads agency:**
"I'm a specialist in helping dentists get new patients using paid ads on Meta. Give three concise and short paid media ideas based on the client's website/description:
- Identify key specials to offer based on what they do
- Unique offer ideas to get new patients in the door
- Strategies to increase leads

Idea 1: Creative idea based on who they target
Idea 2: Targeting idea based on their location
Idea 3: Creative idea based on who they target

Each idea should be conversational, one line, unique and short. Tone: professional, conversational. No bold letters. Start each idea with a dash on a new row."

Input: company description, company website, company location.

**Example output (Claude):**
- Create a campaign targeting parents in Austin with a "first visit fun pack" for kids, including a toy and photo with a tooth fairy character
- Target new Austin residents within 10-mile radius with a "Welcome to Austin" dental package — free exam and discounted cleaning
- Create a campaign for Austin's outdoor enthusiasts featuring a "weekend warrior special" addressing sports-related dental concerns with custom mouthguards

Generate for all contacts in one click. Personalized ideas for every single contact in just a couple of minutes.

## Section 3: The Call to Action — Value, Not Booking

What most people do: "Let's book a call tomorrow at 2 PM. Do you have time?"

**No.** The call to action must also be relevant and value-driven.

Think about it from the customer's perspective: if they're receiving an email and it's just "hey, let's book a call" from someone they don't know — are they going to say yes? Likely not.

### The Value-Based CTA

If you're selling paid ads to dentists, give away something relevant for free:

**Example CTA:** "If you're currently looking for new patients or thinking about running ads in the near future, I'd love to create three proven ads that you could run on your own to start attracting new patients as soon as this week."

### The Full Email (Example)

*Hi Mark,*

*Had some ideas on how you could add some new clients this next quarter based on what's working for our current dentist clients:*

*- Create a campaign targeting young professionals in Austin with a "lunch break smile boost" special offering quick teeth whitening sessions during work hours*

*- Target new Austin residents within 10-mile radius of your practice with a "Welcome to Austin" dental package that includes a free exam and discounted cleaning*

*- Create a campaign for Austin's outdoor enthusiasts featuring a "weekend warrior special" that addresses sports-related dental concerns with custom mouthguards and emergency care education*

*Currently looking for new patients or thinking about running ads in the near future? I'd love to create three proven ads that you could run on your own to start attracting new patients as soon as this week.*

**This is what's getting responses.** If you're looking for new patients, you're going to respond to this and take these people up on their offer.

## The Funnel: From Reply to Booked Call

When someone responds "Yes, I want this," don't just send the deliverable. Here's where the boomerang comes back.

### Why You Don't Just Send the Ads

1. If you're going to create three ads and you know nothing about the business (other than what's on their website), the ads aren't actually going to be helpful or work well
2. There needs to be some skin in the game from both ends — providing maximum value requires understanding their brand, which requires a call

### The Booking Process

When they say yes:
> "Perfect. Let's set up a time to chat so I can understand your brand and create these three ads that are amazing."

### What Happens on the Call

It's essentially a sales call:
- Understand them, understand what they're doing
- Build rapport
- If it leads to "just send me the three ads" — perfect, do that
- But if you have a good sales process and can uncover a problem you can solve as a marketing company, that's naturally going to come up with face time

### The Complete Boomerang Funnel

1. Send relevant email with three ideas + value-based CTA
2. They reply "yes, I want the free ads"
3. Book a call to "understand their brand" so the deliverable is actually good
4. Call becomes a discovery/sales conversation
5. Either deliver the free thing and build relationship, or close the deal on the call

**The mechanism:** Get them from saying yes → call → provide maximum value → create something relevant. Don't just throw them the graphics or landing page. The call is where the real conversion happens.
