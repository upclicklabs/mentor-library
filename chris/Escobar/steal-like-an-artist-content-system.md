# Steal Like an Artist - The Content System

At 23, got fired from a marketing agency. By 25, built a content agency that's done close to a million dollars. All from one platform. All from content I stole.

"Steal from anywhere that resonates with inspiration or fuels your imagination." - Austin Kleon

## Good Theft vs. Bad Theft

**Good theft:** honors, studies, steals from many, credits, transforms, remixes
**Bad theft:** degrades, skims, steals from one, plagiarizes, imitates, rips off

You want to be a good thief.

Six months in, something shifted. Instead of copying what was working, I started noticing what was missing. Everyone was posting the same stuff. But I'd spent months inside a real agency. I'd seen what actually worked for clients. I'd watched founders, heads of growth, CMOs have the same problem over and over that we solved. So I started writing the posts I wanted to read.

## The Testing Sequence

- **Start with text posts.** Fastest to create. Test 10 to 15 hooks. Get used to how to do it before investing in a carousel or video.
- **Double down on winners.** When a text post hits, turn it into a carousel. When a carousel hits, turn it into a video.
- **Track what's working for your specific audience.** The best practices are just starting points. Your data is the truth.
- **Every failure is research.** In the beginning, obscurity is good - when you're unknown, nothing distracts you from getting better.

## What to Collect: Three Sources

Before posting anything, become a collector. Not a hoarder - there's a difference. Hoarders collect indiscriminately. Artists collect selectively.

### 1. Sales Conversations
Sales calls are where you hear the real problems. A prospect says: "We're getting traffic, but it's not converting." "We've been doing SEO for months and can't figure out why we're not ranking." "Our competitors are outranking us and we don't know what they're doing differently."

That's not just sales intel. That's content. Every objection is a post. Every question is a hook. Every frustrated prospect is telling you exactly what to write about.

### 2. Internal Documentation
Don't just read SOPs, strategy docs, onboarding materials - study the thinking behind them. Why did they structure the audit this way? What were they trying to learn? What decisions did this information help them make? When you understand the why, you can explain it. When you can explain it, you have content.

### 3. Expert Interviews
The stuff that's become automatic to subject matter experts is invisible to them. Ask: "Why do you do it that way?" or "What's the mistake you see clients make most often?" Nine times out of ten they'll say something brilliant they'd never think to post because to them it was obvious. That's the gold.

This is the raw material. Everything else is just packaging.

## The Agency Workflow (Steal This)

**Step 1: Record everything.** Have clients record sales calls, strategy sessions, even internal meetings using tools like Fathom.

**Step 2: Mine for insights.** Use a combination of AI and human review to pull out the moments that matter - the surprising stat, the counterintuitive take, the story about a client they forgot was interesting.

**Step 3: Build a content bank.** All insights go into a central repository organized by topic, content type, stage of funnel.

**Step 4: Package and distribute.** Turn raw insights into finished posts - text, carousels, videos, whatever format fits the insight.

This is stealing at scale. Not making stuff up - extracting what already exists in your client's head and putting it where their buyers will see it.

## The Five-Step Framework

**Step 1: Collect.** Before posting anything, become a collector.
- Find 20-30 LinkedIn creators in your space. Study what's working.
- Record your sales calls. Mine them for insights.
- Interview the experts around you. Capture what's obvious to them.
- Document your own processes - the stuff you do without thinking.

**Step 2: Copy.** Start posting.
- Steal formats, structures, hooks that work
- Use the content funnel: Growth, Authority, Conversion, Personal
- Post 3-5 times per week minimum
- Don't try to be 100% original yet - just get reps

**Step 3: Transform.** Start making it yours.
- Notice what topics you keep coming back to
- Identify the takes that feel uniquely yours
- Develop your point of view - what do you believe that others don't?

**Step 4: Adapt.** Pay attention to what's working.
- One platform, one audience, one offer
- Double down on what's hitting
- Adapt what isn't based on your data

**Step 5: Compound.** Let the flywheel spin.
- Consistency beats creativity
- Your old posts keep working
- Reputation compounds faster than followers
- You start building a repurposing machine

## The Core Principle

Collect from everyone who's figured out something you haven't. Study how they think, not just what they do. Mash it all together with your own experience. Then make something that only you could make.

You are not starting from zero. You are starting from everything that's already been figured out. Then adding the one thing nobody else can replicate - your actual experience.
